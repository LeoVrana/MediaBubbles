The grand jury convened by special counsel Robert Mueller in his investigation into possible collusion between the Trump campaign and Russia will soon have the chance to question associates of former national security adviser Mike Flynn. NBC News reports Bijan Kian, an Iranian-American businessman and former board member of the Export-Import bank who once worked at Flynn's now-defunct lobbying firm, the Flynn Intel Group, is a subject of Mueller's probe. Mueller recently indicted former Trump campaign chairman Paul Manafort and a business associate, Rick Gates, on counts including tax fraud and money laundering, tied to their lobbying work. Previous reports have claimed that Mueller's team has enough evidence to indict Flynn and his son, who both worked for the Trump campaign.
