It took a full 24 hours for President Donald Trump to respond to the news that Michael Flynn, his former national security adviser, would cooperate with special counsel Robert Mueller’s investigation as part of a plea deal. But eventually, Trump took to Twitter and claimed he “had to fire General Flynn because he lied to the Vice President and the FBI.” Observers immediately pointed out that Trump had just implied he was aware that Flynn lied to the FBI when the president told James Comey, who met with him the day after Flynn resigned, that he hoped the then-FBI director would be “letting Flynn go.” To some, this strengthened the case against Trump for obstruction of justice.
