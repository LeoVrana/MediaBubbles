There are some things it’s not OK to not know—or know how to do—if you want to be president. You wouldn’t, say, elect a president that didn’t know what a doorknob was or how to use one. While unrelated to the literal functioning of the office of the president—other than awkward bathroom scenarios requiring Secret Service assistance—having a worldview that didn’t factor in the existence of knob technology would be, you know, problematic. Knowledge of how the internet functions is not as fundamental as believing in doorknobs, but having a basic awareness of both is a deal breaker for presidents.
