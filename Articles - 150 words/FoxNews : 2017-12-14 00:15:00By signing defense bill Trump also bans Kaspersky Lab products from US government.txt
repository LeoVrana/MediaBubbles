President Donald Trump signed into law Tuesday legislation that bans the US government from using products created by Kaspersky Lab, a Russian cybersecurity company, amid suspicions it was vulnerable to Kremlin influence. The ban, introduced as part of the annual defense spending bill, codifies a directive issued by the Trump administration in September that federal agencies get rid of Kaspersky Lab’s software, hardware and other services within 90 days, Reuters reported. The ban on Kaspersky Lab products signals growing tensions between Russia and the US and its key allies over fears that the company has ties to Russian intelligence and that its prducts could be used for espionage. “The case against Kaspersky is well documented and deeply concerning.
