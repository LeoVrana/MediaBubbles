The Hillary Clinton presidential campaign as well as the DNC violated campaign finance law when they hid payments to a private firm, a Federal Elections Committee complaint filed Wednesday by a nonpartisan group alleges. The Campaign Legal Center (CLC) claimed the DNC and Hillary for America worked to undermine federal election laws and the public’s right to know after a Washington Post article alleged that a Clinton lawyer hired a private firm to investigate ties between Donald Trump and Russia. “They failed to accurately disclose the purpose and recipient of payments for the dossier of research alleging connections between then-candidate Donald Trump and Russia, effectively hiding these payments from public scrutiny, contrary to the requirements of federal law,” a press release from the organization states.
