The controversial dossier containing salacious allegations about President Donald Trump and his possible connections to Russia, including coordination between the Trump campaign and the Kremlin, resulted from funding by Hilary Clinton's presidential campaign and the Democratic National Committee to the firm Fusion GPS, The Washington Post reported on Tuesday. Fusion GPS was retained by Marc E. Elias, an attorney representing the DNC and the Clinton campaign, The Post reported. Fusion GPS then reportedly hired former British intelligence officer Christopher Steele to write the now-infamous dossier. Steele, a former British intelligence officer, had ties both to the US intelligence community and the FBI. Prior to the firm being retained in April 2016 by Elias and his law firm, Perkins Coie, Fusion GPS’s research had been subsidized by an unidentified Republican during the GOP primary.
