President Trump recently got an ominous signal from former National Security Adviser Mike Flynn’s lawyers: they would no longer discuss Special Counsel Robert Mueller’s investigation with the Trump legal team, after previously sharing information with one another, presumably a sign the early campaign surrogate is cooperating with Mueller’s team and potentially in the process of negotiating a deal. “Defense lawyers frequently share information during investigations, but they must stop when doing so would pose a conflict of interest,” the New York Times notes. “It is unethical for lawyers to work together when one client is cooperating with prosecutors and another is still under investigation.” Flynn, perhaps more than anyone else in Trump World, seemed to be in the most overt legal peril from the outset of the Russia investigation. The retired three-star general had known contacts with Russia, including sitting next to Russian President Vladimir Putin at a 2015 event
