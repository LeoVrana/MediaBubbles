A majority of Americans polled believe there were either definitely or probably improper contacts between Trump officials and Russia during the 2016 campaign, according to a Pew survey out Thursday. Thirty percent of respondents said they believe there was definitely improper communications with Moscow, while another 29 percent said that such contacts probably occurred. Eighteen percent believe there were probably no improper contacts, and 12 percent said there definitely were not. Democrats and Republicans were starkly divided in their beliefs on the issue. Only 27 percent of Republican or Republican-leaning respondents said that they believed Trump officials either definitely or probably had improper contacts with Moscow during the campaign. Among Democratic and Democratic-leaning respondents, that number was 83 percent, according to the Pew poll.
