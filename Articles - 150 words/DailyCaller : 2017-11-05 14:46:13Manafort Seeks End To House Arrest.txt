REUTERS/James Lawler Duggan Former President Donald Trump campaign chairman Paul Manafort will seek more favorable release terms Monday, after a federal court confined him to his Alexandria, Va., home while awaiting trial for twelve criminal charges. Manafort surrendered to the FBI Oct. 30 after special counsel Robert Mueller secured a set of indictments against him for various white-collar crimes. He was released shortly after his arraignment before US Magistrate Judge Deborah Robinson, where he pleaded not guilty to all counts.
