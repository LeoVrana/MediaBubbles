45 After Dark: Not So Full Disclosure edition Last night, he acted like a lot of previous presidents. President Donald Trump’s decision to withholds reams of information about the assassination of former President John F. Kennedy rankled today. Those expecting a full release — as promised by Trump — questioned why Trump was bowing to pressure from the FBI and CIA, POLITICO’s Bryan Bender reports . Story Continued Below “I just don't think there is anything in these records that require keeping them secret now," John Tunheim, the federal judge who oversaw the collection of government documents on John F. Kennedy's assassination told POLITICO. He added that Trump’s decision was “disappointing.” Trump’s decision was in keeping with previous administrations, which have not been forthcoming with the Kennedy documents despite a court ruling that they should be released. There were lots and lots of documents that scholars have craved not included.
