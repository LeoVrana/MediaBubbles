Jeff Sessions (C-SPAN) Two White House official have suggested that Attorney General Jeff Sessions be removed from office so he can be re-appointed to his old Alabama Senate seat to avoid it being filled by disgraced candidate Roy Moore. In a report by the New York Times, two officials who spoke on condition of anonymity said separately that if Sessions were removed from office, Alabama Gov. Kay Ivey of Alabama could immediately re-appoint him. This move would both ensure that Moore does not get elected and that they don’t lose the Republican advantage if Democratic challenger Doug Jones wins the seat.
