(CNN) Former national security adviser Michael Flynn pleaded guilty Friday to lying to the FBI about his contacts with the Russian ambassador during the presidential transition last year. Court documents unsealed on Friday reveal that Flynn's guilty plea stems from a handful of false statements he made to FBI investigators when he was interviewed in late January 2017. The lies were about a series of calls Flynn had with then-Russian Ambassador to the United States Sergey Kislyak. The calls were about two distinct events: a controversial vote at the United Nations and President Barack Obama's decision to punish Russia for meddling in the 2016 US election.
