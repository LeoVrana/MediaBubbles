Mueller blindsides Congress’ Russia investigators Democrats are also concerned Trump will fire the special counsel after indictments of his former campaign aides. The indictments of two former Trump campaign officials and emergence of a third ex-Trump adviser who appears to be cooperating with special counsel Robert Mueller’s investigation into Russian interference in the 2016 election could throw a wrench into Congress' parallel Russia probes. That's in part because neither of Congress' intelligence committees has yet met with George Papadopoulos, a former foreign policy adviser to Donald Trump's campaign who admitted on Monday to lying to the FBI about his efforts to connect Russian entities with Trump's team. It’s unclear whether he or either of the two indicted former campaign officials — Paul Manafort and Rick Gates — can or will continue engaging with the Hill.
